Canadian Canoe hire is available for all types of groups from family holidays planning riverside camping trips, corporate groups undertaking team building programs to stag weekends and hen parties, looking for adventure activities or a survival course.

We offer canoe trips from Glasbury on Wye to Hereford, continuing to Chepstow covering 100 mile of the River Wye, which has something for everyone and various facilities for each type of group. Riverside campsites and B&B’s are available for groups that want to canoe for longer than a day and we have pre-planned sections mapped out in detail highlighting each location and all riverside facilities.

We use ‘Old Town’ canoes which are superb for mixed abilities and experience. They are also large enough to carry all the equipment you will require for your trip. If your looking to plan a river trip or would like more information about canoe hire on the river Wye, contact us today for a friendly chat or read our frequently asked questions page.

Canoeing Weekends

Come and enjoy a relaxing weekend of canoeing or kayaking down the beautiful River Wye. The stunning Wye Valley has something to offer everyone with its beautiful rolling hills, picturesque towns and villages and fantastic selection of hotels, B&B's, campsites and restaurants. Leftbank canoe hire operates along the entire length of the river from Glasbury all the way down to Monmouth enabling many of our regular visitors to come back year after year yet always experience something new.

Sample Canoeing Weekends

Glasbury to Hereford (3days)

Day 1

Glasbury - Highest navigable point on the river in normal summer conditions.
Hay on Wye - Famous for its book shops and a great place for lunch on your first day.
Toll bridge Whitney - Choice of 2 campsites for your first evening and both within walking distance to the local pub

Day 2

Whitney to Preston - Many people consider this to be one of the most enjoyable sections on the River Wye. A slightly more remote and wild feeling stretch which will require stocking up on all the essentials before you set off.
You will be making your way to your next overnight stay at the Wye Camp Here campsite in Preston on Wye, great for both large and small groups. The Yew Tree Pub is only a 15 minute walk up the road.

Day 3

Your final stretch leads you down into the Market town of Hereford, a great place to finish your trip with great tourist attractions and good transport links all over the UK

Hereford to Ross (2/3 days)

Day 1/2

Depending on your time of departure today you could either have a gentle paddle to the Lucksal camping and caravan park for your first night or potentially push on the full 18 miles down to Hoarwithy.
Hoarwithy campsite is a popular stop off for many canoe groups and with the addition of the village pub 300 metres from the river it makes a good place to spend a sunny summers evening.

Day 3

Your final day is spent enjoying the quiet stretch of the river that flows down into Ross-on-Wye. End your weekend at the Hope and Anchor with a refreshing pint and maybe even a Sunday roast.
There are many other Weekends of adventure available on the River Wye, for more information give us a call and let us put together your perfect weekend.

The River Wye Survival Challenge

A two day event which is perfect for corporate team building, stag and hen weekends and groups of friends. Organised in conjunction with the Ultimate Activity Company, the trip is a great way to see the Wye whilst also learning basic survival skills.

The course can run over any two days between March and October.

This challenge includes the four main elements of survival (Fire, Food, Shelter and Water) mixed together with canoeing skills, river navigation and river safety. Each participant has a set amount of equipment, a sixteen-foot open boat “each” to paddle solo for 30 miles down river over two days.

Suggested itinerary: (Example)

Day One:

  • 08:30 - Final kit Check
  • 09:30 - Transport departs Hereford
  • 10:00 - Start day one
  • 16:00 - Arrive at river side camp
  • 17:00 - Build shelters / collect water / cook food

Day Two:

  • 08:30 - Breakfast
  • 09:30 - Break camp
  • 10:00 - Start day two
  • 16:00 - Arrive at Hereford
  • 16:30 - Wet De-brief in local river side bar

Groups meet in Hereford where transport takes you out to your start point. The challenge is to return to Hereford by the following day.

Prices for this activity start at £80 per person. The price includes food, transport, all necessary equipment and an experienced instructor.
